CharacterVoice: Persona-Driven Controllable TTS with Semantic Enhancement

Abstract

0:58: Controllable TTS enables expressive speech synthesis, yet existing methods mainly focus on utterance-level control or simplistic speaker attributes, struggling to map holistic speaker descriptions to coherent speech. To bridge this gap, we propose CharacterVoice, a persona-driven framework that synthesizes customized speech from character profiles. Moving beyond acoustic-centric designs, CharacterVoice adopts persona-centric modeling conditioned on describable traits, aligning synthesis more intuitively with human intent. To capture vocal correlations and ensure stylistic coherence, we integrate instruction-level BERT embeddings as a semantic-enhanced prior. To address the scarcity of annotated data, we introduce a character-anchored annotation pipeline that leverages LLMs’ knowledge of well-known characters to link transient acoustic expressions with abstract personas. Experiments demonstrate that CharacterVoice outperforms baselines in both instruction adherence and naturalness.
