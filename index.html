CharacterVoice: Persona-Driven Controllable TTS via High-Level Semantic Modeling

Abstract

While controllable Text-to-Speech (TTS) has advanced significantly, most systems still rely on low-level acoustic features or reference audio. This acoustic-centric paradigm lacks a profound understanding of high-level semantic character representations, restricting users from intuitively designing unseen voices based on conceptual personas. To bridge the semantic gap between abstract character profiles and acoustic manifestations, we propose \textbf{CharacterVoice}, a semantic-driven TTS framework that synthesizes customized speech directly from multi-dimensional natural language descriptions (e.g., personality, social identity). To address the scarcity of complex paired data, we design a highly scalable, LLM-empowered annotation pipeline that extracts thousands of stable persona instructions from movie speakers with minimal manual effort. Furthermore, we introduce a comprehensive Gemini-based automated benchmark to evaluate open-ended semantic control. Experiments demonstrate that our system achieves excellent instruction-following capabilities and high naturalness in persona creation.
